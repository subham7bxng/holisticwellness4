Reiki Healing

Reiki healing is a spiritual practice that originated in Japan in the early 20th century. It involves the transfer of universal energy from the practitioner's palms to their patient's body, promoting emotional or physical healing. The word "Reiki" is derived from two Japanese words: "rei," meaning universal, and "ki," meaning life energy or vital force.

Distance Reiki Healing

Distance Reiki healing, also known as remote Reiki or absent Reiki, is a practice where Reiki energy is sent across space and time to a recipient who is not physically present with the practitioner. This form of Reiki is based on the belief that energy is not bound by physical limitations and can be directed to anyone, anywhere in the world.

The process of distance Reiki typically involves the practitioner and the recipient arranging a specific time for the session, although it can also be sent without a scheduled appointment. The recipient may choose to relax in a quiet space during the session, but it's not necessary for them to be in a meditative state or consciously focusing on the healing.

The practitioner may use various techniques to facilitate distance Reiki, such as visualization, intention setting, or using a surrogate object to represent the recipient. They channel Reiki energy just as they would during an in-person session, directing it to the recipient with the intention of healing, balancing, and harmonizing their energy.

About Reiki Healing

During a Reiki session, the practitioner places their hands lightly on or just above the recipient's body, in various positions. The goal is to channel this healing energy to areas where it is needed most. The recipient may feel sensations such as warmth, tingling, or relaxation during the session.

Reiki is often used to promote relaxation, reduce stress, and support the body's natural ability to heal itself. While it is considered a holistic practice and not a replacement for conventional medical treatment, many people find it beneficial for overall well-being and as a complementary therapy.

The practice of Reiki is based on the idea that imbalances or blockages in the flow of life force energy can contribute to physical, emotional, or spiritual issues. By restoring balance and facilitating the free flow of energy, Reiki aims to support healing on multiple levels.
